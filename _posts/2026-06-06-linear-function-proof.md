In the linear function $y = ax + b$, **a** is **slope**. 
So, in this post I'll prove that a is slope

## what is slope?
In mathematics, the slope of a line is defined as 

$$ \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} $$

## The proof
Let's choose any two different arbitrary points on linear function $y = ax + b$ .

And let P and Q be two arbitrary points on the function.
$$P(x_1,y_1), Q(x_2,y_2)$$

Let's substitute these points into the function

$$ y_1 = ax_1 + b $$

$$ y_2 = ax_2 + b $$

and lets Subtract $y_1$ from $y_2$. It will be

$$ y_2 - y_1 = (ax_2 +b) - (ax_1 + b) $$

$$ y_2 - y_1 = ax_2 - ax_1 $$

$$ y_2 - y_1 = a(x_2-x_1) $$

If we Divide both sides by (x_2 - x_1)

$$ \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} = a $$

$$ a = \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} $$
